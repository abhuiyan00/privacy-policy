This Privacy Policy explains how personal data is handled when you visit this website or use this project (the “Service”). It is written to align with the EU/UK General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR/UK GDPR) and the California Consumer Privacy Act as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CCPA/CPRA).

Plain-language summary. This is a personal, non-commercial project. It does not run analytics, advertising, or third-party trackers, and it does not set its own cookies. The only data handled is (1) standard server logs kept by the hosting provider for security, and (2) anything you choose to send by email. Personal data is never sold or shared for advertising. You have full rights over your data.

3. Key terms

  • Personal data — information relating to an identifiable person (name, email, IP address).
  • Processing — any operation on personal data (collecting, storing, using, deleting).
  • Controller — the party that decides why and how data is processed.
  • Processor — a party that processes data on the controller’s behalf (e.g. the host).

This Service does not set its own cookies and does not use analytics, advertising, fingerprinting, or other tracking by default. If cookies are ever introduced, this policy will be updated first and, where the law requires it, your consent will be requested. You can control cookies through your browser settings.

7. Hosting and third-party services

This Service is hosted on GitHub Pages (GitHub, Inc.). As the host, GitHub acts as a processor and may collect visitor information — such as IP addresses and server logs — to operate the platform, maintain security, and meet legal obligations. This is standard for web hosting and outside our control. See the GitHub Privacy Statement and the GitHub Pages data-collection notice. No other third-party processors are used by default.

8. How we share information — and selling/sharing

We do not sell your personal information and do not share it for cross-context behavioural advertising (CCPA/CPRA). In the preceding 12 months we have not sold or shared personal information. We disclose data only to service providers strictly to run the Service, when required by law, to protect rights and safety, or with your consent. Because we neither sell nor share, no “Do Not Sell or Share” link is required.

9. Your privacy rights

To exercise any right, email farhanheisenberg360@gmail.com; we respond within the legal timeframe (generally 30 days under GDPR, 45 days under CCPA/CPRA) and never discriminate against you for exercising your rights.

EEA / UK (GDPR)

Access; rectification; erasure; restriction; objection; portability; withdraw consent; and lodge a complaint with your supervisory authority (in Poland, UODO).

California (CCPA/CPRA)

Know; access; delete; correct; opt out of sale/sharing (not applicable here); limit use of sensitive information (none collected); and non-discrimination. You may use an authorised agent.

Other jurisdictions

Where laws such as Brazil’s LGPD or Canada’s PIPEDA grant similar rights, we honour them.

10. International data transfers

The Service is hosted on infrastructure that may process data outside your country, including the United States. Where data leaves the EEA/UK, the host relies on recognised safeguards such as the EU Standard Contractual Clauses.

11. Data retention

Emails are kept only as long as needed to handle your request, then deleted. Server logs are retained by the host under its own schedule, typically for a short period. When data is no longer needed it is deleted or anonymised.

12. Data security

We rely on the host’s security (HTTPS in transit, platform hardening, access controls). No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, but we take reasonable steps to protect any data we handle.

13. Children’s privacy

This Service is not directed to children under 13 (or the minimum age of digital consent in your jurisdiction, which is 16 in parts of the EU). We do not knowingly collect data from children; contact us and we will delete any such data.

14. Changes to this policy
